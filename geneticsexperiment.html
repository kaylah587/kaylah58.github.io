What are genetics? And what makes people similar or different from one another? Those were my questions going into this experiment. To begin this unit, our teacher Mx.Tyler presented us with a portion of a documentary focussing on a scientist named Rosalind Franklin who is most famous for her contribution to the discovery of the double helix. By using X-ray Crystallography, Rosalind was able to get a picture, famously known as, “photo 51” which revealed the double helix’s structure. After learning the background information on DNA, we got to do some hands-on activities and experiment on what we thought the formation of DNA looks like. With our newfound knowledge, we finally began learning what our experiment would be. We learned that there were four major parts to this experiment…

  1. Create an interview with our group members answering questions about what we think the results of the experiment would be
  2. Do the experiment
  3. Create a concluding interview on our results and finding after completing the experiment
  4. Make a documentary to present what we did in the experiment

    5. The point of this experiment is to collect our DNA and through measuring how long it is, determine who our DNA matches with the most throughout our entire freshman class.
